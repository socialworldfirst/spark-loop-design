The Spark Loop

You hand over a project brief and an end goal. The loop drives your existing spark machine from that brief to that goal and stops. It reads nothing else: no scan, no meetings, no bank. The outcome is whatever the brief says it is, shipped as spoken-word scripts by default.

Revised 9 June 2026 · v2 brief-driven Form: /loop until <goal> <brief> Default output: spoken scripts

01 The signature

The whole mental model is two arguments. One says what to make it from, the other says when it is done.

loop ⟨project brief⟩ ⟨end goal⟩
project brief
You provide it before the loop starts. It is the only source of the outcome: topic, audience, pillars, constraints, the angle to take. If it is not in the brief, it does not enter the work.
end goal
The stop condition. What "done" looks like as a concrete artifact, for example twelve spoken-word reel scripts in a talent-read doc. The loop converges on it, then ends.
This is the answer to your first question, loop or goal. They are not rivals. The goal is the second argument. The loop is what carries the brief to it. /loop until is the exact shape: a finite job with a finish line you state up front.

02 What the loop sits on

Brief in on the left, goal out on the right, your existing run state machine in the middle. Nothing feeds the front except you.

The brief lands in the run's seed_context and alignment fields, which already exist. The end goal becomes a machine-checkable end_goal field. Four gates stay amber (your call), the work between them stays green (Claude's to run). The loop ticks the machine until the goal artifact exists, then stops on its own.

Anatomy of the run machine, stage by stage

Those six dots are not labels, they are six real stages with a strict rhythm: an amber gate (your decision) always brackets a green auto leg (Claude's to run), and the last auto leg lands on the goal. Three of your decisions, three of Claude's drives, interleaved. Here is what actually happens at each, the real sub-skill behind it, and the run.json state it sits in.

  1. Idea boardGATE 1 · you decide
    /spark_idea turns your brief into an angle board: a deployed HTML page of angles spread across your pillars. You tick the ones that are on-brand and worth pursuing, comment, flag "find more like this", and paste back. Claude iterates the board v2, v3, until you say "wrap". This is a human gate because it is brand fit and editorial taste, the one thing a heuristic should not decide for you. In: the brief. Out: a wrapped handoff doc listing the angles you kept. · stage=idea_board → idea_iterating · open_gate=gate1_angle_picks
  2. Listen + produceAUTO · Claude drives
    Two auto sub-stages, no stops. /spark_listen clusters the angles you kept and runs deep multi-agent research per cluster (on the shopify run this was 5 clusters pulling real numbers: Shopify's 1.5 to 2% conversion fee, bank FX margins of 2 to 4%, the $800M hidden-FX figure, FCA safeguarding). Then the production-cards page is built straight from those research briefs, one card per researched angle, and deployed. Claude runs the whole leg without asking. In: your kept angles. Out: research briefs plus a live cards page. · stage=listen → produce · open_gate=null
  3. CardsGATE 2 · you decide
    On the cards page you pick which researched angles are strong enough to become scripts, drop comments, paste back. This is a separate gate from the first because research changes things: an angle that looked great in the brief can deflate once the facts are in, and a quiet one can become the best card after research backs it. You are judging the angles with the evidence now attached. In: the cards page. Out: the picked angle set that goes to script. · stage=produce · open_gate=gate2_produce_picks
  4. Spoken scriptsAUTO · Claude drives
    /spark_script writes each picked angle as a pure spoken-word voiceover script grounded in its own research brief, runs the hook engine, and builds the variant page. Your standing rule applies here: spoken-word talent-read copy is the default treatment, and the live /voice_picker creator-search path stays off unless the brief asked for it. This is the leg that produces the words your talent will read. In: picked angles plus research. Out: drafted spoken scripts, usually a few variants per idea. · stage=script · open_gate=null
  5. Pick winnerGATE 3 · you decide
    On the variant page you pick the one script that ships per idea and paste back. The last taste call: which hook lands, which read is tightest, which version sounds like WorldFirst out loud. In the old design there was a fourth gate here for choosing the voice-polish engine. That gate is gone, because spoken scripts are now automatic and voice picker is a one-word opt-in, not a stop. In: the script variants. Out: one winning script per idea. · stage=voice_iterate · open_gate=gate3_voice_picks
  6. Talent-read docAUTO · Claude drives
    Claude assembles the winning scripts into one talent-read Google Doc with locked spoken-word styling, sets it to anyone-with-link viewer, and logs it to your inbox. This is the terminal artifact, the thing your talent actually reads from, and the thing the end-goal check is looking for. In: the winners. Out: the final doc plus its share link. · stage=final → done · open_gate=null
The END GOAL box is not a stage, it is a test. Your end goal is stored as a checkable spec, for example {deliverable:"talent_read_doc", scripts:12, voice:"spoken"}. After the doc stage, every tick the loop asks one question of reality: does that doc exist, does it hold 12 scripts, are they spoken. The instant all three are true the loop sends the final link and ends, with nothing left running. That green tick under the box is the whole reason this is a goal-bounded loop and not a standing process: it has a finish line, and it knows when it has crossed it.
In autonomous mode the rhythm collapses. If your brief set autonomous_run:true, the three amber gates self-clear by heuristic (top angles by fit per pillar at GATE 1, keep the researched winners at GATE 2, ship the spoken scripts at GATE 3) and all six stages run start to finish unattended to the exact same doc. The machine does not change, only who answers at the amber dots.

03 The form and cadence

On demand, not on a clock. You start it by handing over the brief. It self-paces from there and ends when the goal is met. No cron, because there is no standing reservoir to maintain any more.

Self-paced · ScheduleWakeup

Between ticks the loop sets its own next wake. Fast (~270s, inside the prompt cache) while a research or script workflow is running. Long while it is parked at a gate waiting on you. It is the only timer.

Bounded · until <end goal>

Every tick checks the goal. The moment the target artifact exists (say the talent-read doc holds the target script count) the loop stops and sends one final line with the link. A real finish line, stated up front.

Why this beats the old cron design. The first version was a standing controller holding bank and run buffers full forever, so it needed a heartbeat. A brief-and-goal job is finite, so a self-paced until loop fits perfectly and there is nothing to schedule, supervise, or leave running after the goal lands.

04 The two rules that bound it

These are the constraints you set. They are not preferences inside the loop, they are walls around it.

Rule 1 · brief only

It reads your brief and nothing else

No /spark_scan, no meetings ingest, no session sweep, no Content Bank lookup. The outcome is whatever the brief says it should be. The loop never goes hunting for material. You are the front of the pipe.

spark_scan meetings sessions bank
Rule 2 · spoken by default

Every script ships as spoken-word

Talent-read spoken scripts are the default treatment for every run. The live /voice_picker creator-search path stays off, it is the route that stalled at ~2M tokens on the shopify run. It runs only when you say "use voice picker" for that brief.

default spoken scripts opt-in only: voice picker

05 The per-tick reconcile

One tick is one pass of this list, scoped to the one run the brief created. It is idempotent: nothing to do means it notifies no one and either sleeps or stops.

  1. Load this run's state. Read the brief's run.json only. No scan, no bank, no other runs. Where are we, which gate is open, is the goal met yet.
  2. Advance auto-work.green If the run is on an auto stage (open_gate=null), drive the next leg. Heavy stages (listen+produce, scripts) fire the existing multi-agent workflow, token-budgeted.
  3. Default to spoken scripts. At the script stage, produce spoken-word talent-read copy. Branch to /voice_picker only if the brief or you explicitly asked for it. Otherwise the live path never runs.
  4. Park and notify, or auto-clear.amber At a gate: in gated mode send one DingTalk line via the kao bot and wait. In autonomous mode, self-clear it (see the dial) and keep driving. Dedupe so a gate pings once.
  5. Check the end goal.stop Test the end_goal against reality: does the target artifact exist with the target shape (e.g. doc present, 12 scripts, spoken). If yes, ship the final link and end the loop.
  6. Budget guard. Cap tokens per tick. A research+script workflow runs ~1.3M. If the next action would blow the cap, defer to the next tick, log it, ping only if it blocks you.
  7. Pick the next wake. Workflow running, self-schedule ~270s. Parked at a gate, sleep long. Goal met, do not reschedule at all.

06 The autonomy dial

The one choice left, set per run when you hand over the brief. Your run.json already carries an autonomous_run flag and the shopify run proved it works end to end.

Default · gated

The loop stages, you curate

Drives every auto leg and parks at the gates. You pick angles at GATE 1, winners at GATE 2, the winning script at GATE 3. The loop removes the nudging and the cold start, you keep the taste calls. Scripts still ship spoken by default.

GATE 1 pick angles GATE 2 pick winners GATE 3 pick script
Opt-in · autonomous

Hand over the brief, walk away

Set autonomous_run:true in the brief and the loop self-clears its gates: top angles by fit per pillar, keep the researched winners, ship the spoken scripts straight to the talent-read doc. End to end to the goal, no stops, no live voice path.

use for a tight brief gate when you want taste in the loop
Gate 4 from the old design is gone from the default path. Voice polish is no longer a decision: spoken scripts ship automatically, and voice picker is a one-word opt-in, not a gate.

07 Worked example

You hand over a brief and state the goal. Gated mode, spoken scripts (no voice picker).

Brief (you provide): WorldFirst x Shopify get-paid. Audience: cross-border Shopify sellers. Four pillars (payment pain, hidden FX, put the money to work, trust and scale). Short-form first, English only.
End goal: 12 spoken-word reel scripts in one talent-read Google Doc.
You run: loop ⟨that brief⟩ ⟨12 spoken scripts, talent-read doc⟩
Tick 1 · kickoff
Brief in, board out. No scan.

The loop reads only the brief. Seeds the run from it (seed_context, alignment, end_goal). Builds the idea board, 24 angles across your four pillars, deploys it, parks GATE 1, pings you. Goal not met. Parked on you, so it schedules a long wake.

Tick 2 · you picked 12 angles at GATE 1
It catches the clear and drives

Run is now on auto. Fires the research+produce workflow (the 17-agent pattern, budgeted), builds the cards page, parks GATE 2, pings. Workflow ran mid-tick, so it self-schedules a 270s recheck.

Tick 3 · you picked the winners at GATE 2
Spoken scripts, no voice picker

Drafts the picked angles as spoken-word talent-read scripts (the default, the live voice path stays off because you did not ask for it). Builds the variant page, parks GATE 3 for the winning script per idea, pings.

Tick 4 · you picked at GATE 3
Goal met, loop ends

Assembles the 12 spoken scripts into one talent-read Google Doc, anyone-with-link viewer, logged to inbox. Checks end_goal: doc exists, 12 scripts, spoken. True. Sends the final line with the link and stops. Nothing left running.

Had the brief said autonomous, ticks 2 to 4 collapse into one unattended run to the same doc. Had you said use voice picker, tick 3 would route the picked scripts through /voice_picker before the doc. Default leaves both off.

08 What to actually build

Smaller than the old design. No cron, no reconcile-the-reservoir script. The loop is an on-demand until run plus a goal check.

  1. The invocation. loop ⟨project brief⟩ ⟨end goal⟩ maps to a self-paced /loop until <goal met> seeded by the brief. A thin /spark_loop wrapper can take the two arguments and set it up.
  2. New run.json fields. brief (or a path to it), end_goal (a machine-checkable spec, e.g. {deliverable:"talent_read_doc", scripts:12, voice:"spoken"}), voice_mode (spoken default or voice_picker), plus last_notified_gate to dedupe pings.
  3. A goal-check function. goal_met(run.json, end_goal) returns true/false. This is the until evaluator and the only new logic of substance.

Default the dial to gated and the voice to spoken. The minimal v1 can even skip the workflow-firing and just stage the idea board to GATE 1 and notify, which already proves the brief-to-board path. Add the drain side and the goal check once the parking and the spoken-script default feel right.
